Simple Meat Recipe

Delight in the rich flavors of this mouthwatering grilled meat, marinated with a blend of spices and herbs for several hours to ensure a delicious taste. This recipe is perfect for serving as a main course at gatherings or special occasions. Pair the grilled meat with roasted vegetables and a fresh salad to complete your meal. Don’t forget to add a special sauce to enhance the flavor. Get ready to enjoy it with family and friends!

    Preparation Time
  • Total: Approximately 30 minutes
  • Preparation: 10 minutes
  • Cooking: 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of butter or oil
  • 1 glass of water

Instructions

  1. Choose Your Cut and Prepare: Select a cut of meat suitable for your recipe (e.g., steaks, roasts, or ground meat). Trim any excess fat and season it with salt, pepper, and any other spices or marinades you prefer. Allow it to sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes to ensure even cooking.
  2. Preheat the Cooking Surface : Whether you’re using a grill, oven, or stovetop, preheat your cooking surface to the appropriate temperature. For grilling, aim for medium-high heat (about 400-450°F or 200-230°C). For roasting, preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  3. Sear the Meat : If cooking on the stovetop or grill, sear the meat for a few minutes on each side until it develops a nice brown crust. This helps lock in the juices and adds flavor. Use a bit of oil to prevent sticking, if necessary.
  4. Cook to the Right Temperature : Use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature. Different types of meat have different safe cooking temperatures: Beef, lamb, and pork (steaks, roasts):
    • 145°F (63°C) for medium-rare
    • 160°F (71°C) for medium.
    • Chicken and turkey: 165°F (74°C).
    • Ground meats: 160°F (71°C).
  5. Rest the Meat: After cooking, let the meat rest for about 5-10 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making it more tender and flavorful. Cover it loosely with foil to keep warm while resting.
